Blood Donation Guidelines & Health Benefits

Learn about why blood donation is important and what you should do before/after donating blood.

Health Benefits of Donating

  • Revitalizes Blood Cells: Donating blood stimulates the production of new red blood cells, keeping your cardiovascular system healthy.
  • Free Health Checkup: Every time you donate, you get a mini-physical checkup including blood pressure, pulse, temperature, and hemoglobin levels.
  • Saves up to 3 Lives: A single blood donation can be split into its components (red cells, plasma, platelets) to save multiple patients.

Pre-Donation Guidelines

What to DO:
  • Drink plenty of water (500ml) or non-alcoholic fluids before donating.
  • Eat a healthy, low-fat meal. Do not donate on an empty stomach.
  • Get a good night's sleep (7-8 hours) prior to donation.
What to AVOID:
  • Do NOT consume alcohol within 24 hours prior to donation.
  • Avoid fatty foods like burgers or fries, as they can interfere with blood testing.
  • Do NOT smoke for at least 4 hours before your donation.

Post-Donation Guidelines

What to DO:
  • Drink extra fluids throughout the day to replace lost volume.
  • Keep the strip bandage on for at least 4-6 hours.
  • Eat iron-rich foods (spinach, beans, raisins, poultry) after donation.
What to AVOID:
  • Do NOT lift heavy objects or do intense exercise for 24 hours.
  • Avoid standing for long periods or going into direct hot sun.
  • Do NOT consume alcohol for at least 24 hours post-donation.
